Physicist · Engineer · Inventor

Instruments and interfaces across physics, textiles, and computation.

Three decades of work in physical computing, sensing, and fabrication — from microscopic sculpture and magnetic levitation to wearable computers and under-display cameras.

Current work

Raven — a wearable ambient computer

Since 2024, building Raven: an all-day wearable ambient computing device with a full-color display and Linux on a 64-bit ARM processor, controlled hands-free by eye and voice.

Selected work

01

Camera Behind the Display

2020–2022

Reconstructing clear images from a camera mounted behind a phone's active screen — computationally undoing the diffraction of the display's own pixel grid.

02

TriboTouch

2013–2019

Passive triboelectric touch sensing — a single electrode reads the charge displaced on contact, registering a bare finger, a gloved hand, or a plastic stylus alike.

03

Sandcastles

2010–2012

Microscopic castles carved onto individual grains of sand with a focused ion beam.

04

ZeroN Levitated Interaction Element

2011

A physical mass held and moved through mid-air by a computer-controlled magnetic field.

05

Sp4rkl3

2008

A skirt that lights itself from the triboelectric charge of its own motion — no batteries.

06

Particle Trap IMU

2003

A six-degree-of-freedom inertial measurement unit — the doctoral work — that reads motion from a single micro-bead trapped in an electric field and watched through a microscope.

07

Pengachu

2000–2001

A cheap, open, wireless Linux palmtop for education — a precursor to the OLPC XO laptop.

08

Multitouch Table at MoMA

1999

An early interactive multi-touch surface, exhibited at the Museum of Modern Art years before touchscreens were everywhere.

09

Musical MIDI Jacket

1997

A denim jacket with a musical keypad embroidered straight into the fabric — the master's work, and one of the first true e-textiles; now in the London Science Museum's permanent collection.
