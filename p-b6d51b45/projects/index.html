Projects

A body of work in physical computing, sensing, and fabrication — grouped by theme. See current work for Raven.

Computational imaging & optics

OpenFINCH

2022–2024
An open-source computational-holography platform — reconstructing images with a spatial light modulator and cheap open hardware. An optics lab for pennies on the dollar.

Camera Behind the Display

2020–2022
Real-time image-deblurring algorithms that restore clean pictures from a camera hidden behind an active display, by deconvolving the display's point-spread function.

Interaction & sensing

TriboTouch

2013–2019
Passive triboelectric touch sensing that reads the charge displaced on contact — sensing gloves, styluses, and bare fingers alike from a single electrode.

FakeyMakey

2012
A hand-milled take on the MaKey MaKey — a USB board that turns everyday conductive objects into keyboard and mouse inputs through alligator-clip pads.

Multitouch Table at MoMA

1999
Interactive multitouch table installation at the Museum of Modern Art.

Intrabody Networking

1997–2023
Moving data and power through the human body by capacitive coupling — a 23-year throughline from the MIT Media Lab to a modern physical layer at Samsung.

Inertial sensing

High-Definition IMU

2007–2009
A hardened, high-resolution wireless IMU for gait analysis — reconstructing each limb's stride trajectory by constrained integration of on-body inertial data.

Haltere IMU

2009–2010
A bio-inspired vibrating-structure gyroscope — a driven copper-foil resonator that senses rotation the way a fly's haltere does.

Particle Trap IMU

2003
Novel inertial measurement unit using a suspended metal bead in an electric field.

MEMS Interferometric Accelerometer

Fall 1999
Novel microfabricated accelerometer based on optical interferometry principles.

Wearables & e-textiles

µTartan

2009
A macroscale experiment in patterning circuits into woven textile — chip footprints, traces, and coils formed in conductive cloth at millimetre scale.

Interactive Tablecloth

2009
An ordinary table covering woven with spiral capacitive sensors — feeling touch and the footprint of objects placed on it.

Sp4rkl3

Jan 2008
A dress that lights up using electricity generated by its own motion, with no batteries required.

j4k3t 2.0

Feb 2006
Evolution of wearable electronic interfaces, building on the foundation of the Musical MIDI Jacket.

Stretchable Cable

2005, Sep
Novel arrangement of non-elastic elements in a geometry that can extend to several times its resting length.

E-broidery

1997–2000
Building electronic circuits in cloth — embroidering conductive thread into washable fabric to make soft wires, sensors, and keypads.

MIDI Jacket

1997
Pioneering wearable computing project integrating technology directly into clothing.

Wearable Computing

1995 - 2000
Early research and development in always-on, personal computing devices at the MIT Media Lab.

HackMan 0.4

1995–1996
A self-built wearable computer — a 25 MHz 486 Linux box in a fanny pack, driving a heads-up display and typed one-handed.

Levitation & micro-fabrication

ZeroN levitated interaction element

2011
A new tangible interaction element that can be levitated and moved freely by computer in 3D space.

Sandcastles

2010-2012
Microscopic castles etched onto individual grains of sand using a focused ion beam microscope.

Functional Digital Materials

2009
Building structures by assembling many small discrete parts from a limited set of types — 'micro legos' for a digital approach to fabrication.

Open computing & networking

Pengachu

2000–2001
A cheap, open, wireless Linux palmtop for education — an OLPC precursor built at the MIT Media Lab.

Hello Radio

2009
A low-cost milled-PCB wireless transceiver — up to 256 kbps and 1 km range — from the MIT Center for Bits and Atoms.
